## CSC and Ceph Foundation Board Meeting

The Ceph Foundation Board recently hosted the Ceph Steering Committee (CSC) for a collaborative discussion focused on the current state of the Ceph project and what’s needed to move it forward.

This was the first quarterly meeting, designed to foster communication between those building Ceph (the CSC) and those operating, using, and contributing to it. The Ceph Foundation leaders’ goal is not to influence the CSC but rather to understand its mission and offer support for its goals, thereby bridging the communication gap. The meeting's agenda and notes are available [here](https://pad.ceph.com/p/foundation-board-csc-meeting).

### Key discussion areas

- Operational complexity, especially during failure  
- Friction in contributing and getting reviews  
- Fragmented communication  
- Unclear strategy in some areas  
- Unclear ownership across parts of the ecosystem  

### Major takeaways

- Closing the gap between developer experience and real-world operator needs, especially around failure handling and upgrades.
- Contributor experience remains a key challenge, with CI complexity and limited reviewer bandwidth identified as the biggest sources of friction, not the contribution process itself.
- The community acknowledged that perception matters: even when unintentional, feedback that Ceph feels “unwelcoming” is being taken seriously and will inform improvements to onboarding and engagement.
- While Ceph has clear technical focus areas, such as performance and efficiency, there is an opportunity to better communicate project-wide priorities to align contributors and ecosystem partners. 

### Action items

- **Ceph Community Manager**: Document best practices for large contributions requiring early community engagement
- **Ceph Community Manager**: Improve discoverability of contributor guidelines and ambassador resources 
- **Foundation**: Complete technical writer hiring by the end of April 2025
- **CSC**: Prepare Q2 response on project priorities, pain points, and foundation delegation opportunities
- **Ceph Community Manager**: Share contributor feedback data with CSC for deeper analysis

## Road to Linux Foundation Onboarding: Charters Approved

Ceph now has a clearly defined governance model that separates technical decision-making from funding and community growth, making it easier to understand how decisions are made and how to get involved. This task began in September 2025, when the Ceph Foundation initiated its transition into The Linux Foundation, marking a significant step toward strengthening the long-term sustainability and neutrality of the Ceph project. 

The outcome of this work was the formal approval of two foundational governance frameworks. The Ceph Foundation Charter was established to define how the Foundation raises, allocates, and manages resources in support of the project, while also creating a clear and transparent structure for decision-making, community outreach, and ecosystem development. At its core, the Charter exists to ensure that Ceph operates as a vendor-neutral, community-driven project with sustainable funding and broad industry participation.

In parallel, the Ceph Technical Charter was approved by the Ceph Steering Committee (CSC), reinforcing the independence of the project’s technical governance and clarifying how technical decisions are made in alignment with the needs of the community.

Together, these milestones establish a balanced governance model: the Foundation focuses on funding, outreach, and ecosystem growth, while the technical community retains authority over the project’s direction and innovation.

The third and final step of this process, which is currently in the works, involves transferring the Ceph trademark to the Linux Foundation after completing steps one and two.

**Learn more:**
- [Foundation Charter](https://cdn.platform.linuxfoundation.org/agreements/cephfoundation.pdf) 
- [Technical Charter](https://github.com/ceph/ceph/blob/main/doc/technical-charter.rst)

## OVHcloud Spending Update

The Ceph Governing Board, in collaboration with OVHcloud, a Ceph Foundation member, is making significant strides in reducing infrastructure costs. Mark Nelson (Clyso, Co-Founder and Head of R&D) announced this exciting cross-collaboration, which is freeing up thousands of dollars monthly. This saved funding will be directly reinvested, significantly boosting the Foundation's capacity to support initiatives such as community programs, events, and infrastructure.

### Current overview

- Monthly costs ranged between $5K–$10K USD over the past year  
- Peak spending occurred around August  
- Current costs are approximately $7K/month  

### Key cost drivers

- Storage is the primary expense  
- Four 10TB volumes and associated snapshots accounted for over $4K/month  

### Recent actions

- Unused snapshots and resources have been removed by David Galloway (IBM, Ceph Engineering)
- Ongoing collaboration with OVHcloud to identify additional optimization opportunities
- Further cost savings are expected as usage is refined
 

### What’s next

- Continued monitoring and cost optimization efforts
- Improved visibility into infrastructure usage and spending
- Updates will be shared in the next newsletter and monthly reports as new efficiencies are realized

## Upcoming Ceph Events and the State of Cephalocon

The Ceph Foundation is evolving its approach to events in order to better serve the global community.

Rather than concentrating resources on a single large event like Cephalocon, the Foundation is prioritizing local and regional engagement to expand Ceph’s reach across adjacent communities and projects. This decision came after receiving newly formatted rules about spending within the foundation, these rules came from Linux Foundation board members. 

After seven years of hosting Cephalocon, the Ceph Foundation is shifting its event strategy to prioritize more local, community-driven engagement over a single large annual event.

Due to the increasingly high cost concentration with hosting Cephalocon over the past seven years, the Ceph Foundation has decided it can no longer afford to continue hosting the event.

### What’s changing

- Increased focus on **[Ceph Days](https://ceph.io/en/community/events/), meetups, [data-driven conferences](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/), and community-led events**
- Strategic investment in opportunities that introduce Ceph to new audiences
- A shift toward distributed, community-driven engagement
  
### Future of Cephalocon

- The Foundation remains open to hosting large-scale events like Cephalocon
- However, under current fiduciary guidelines, **broader community support and sponsorship participation are required**
- This ensures alignment with the Foundation’s financial responsibilities while enabling sustainable growth
  

### How to get involved

- As part of the Foundation’s financial stewardship under the Linux Foundation, large-scale events require broader community sponsorship and participation.
- Organize or support a Ceph event in your region
- Partner with related open source or data infrastructure communities


### Need support?

- Community members can [submit funding requests](https://form.asana.com/?k=7aCHVRhp0x1Ga1nOCXlckQ&d=9283783873717) to attend or organize events
- Requests are reviewed by the Board (approval is not guaranteed)
